Hotspot 3: North East

Detailed Climate Analysis and Impact on Agriculture

Regional Climate Analysis

North-East India, comprising ecologically rich yet fragile states, experiences significant climate changes manifesting as increased temperatures, variable rainfall, and more extreme weather events. Recent climate analyses reveal a marked rise in annual mean temperatures and altered monsoon patterns, resulting in heavier rainfall intensity with reduced rainy days. The region faces increased vulnerability to flooding, landslides, and soil erosion, impacting its predominantly agricultural economy. Agriculture, dominated by rice, horticulture, spices, and tea plantations, encounters substantial challenges. Irregular monsoon patterns disrupt traditional cultivation schedules, affecting crop yield and quality. Frequent floods and landslides lead to significant crop loss, soil degradation, and disruption of transportation and market access, exacerbating socio-economic vulnerability. Tea plantations, crucial to the regional economy, witness reduced productivity and altered crop quality due to increased temperatures and erratic rainfall. Adaptation strategies tailored for North-East India encompass promoting climate-resilient agricultural practices, sustainable land management, and disaster risk reduction. Emphasis on agroforestry, mixed cropping, and organic farming practices helps conserve biodiversity, improve soil health, and sustain livelihoods. Indigenous knowledge systems, combined with modern scientific advancements, play a crucial role in adaptive agricultural planning. Infrastructure improvements, including better drainage systems, slope stabilization, and flood-resistant crop varieties, are essential to counter increased climatic risks. Government initiatives aimed at climate resilience, including enhancing meteorological forecasting capabilities, establishing climate information centers, and conducting farmer training programs, are crucial. Encouraging crop diversification, enhancing post-harvest infrastructure, and creating supportive market systems for climate-resilient produce further strengthen agriculture sustainability and economic resilience in North-East India.

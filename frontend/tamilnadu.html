Hotspot 2: Tamil Nadu

Detailed Climate Analysis and Impact on Agriculture

Regional Climate Analysis

Tamil Nadu's climate has shown significant alterations, notably increasing temperatures and shifting rainfall patterns. The state is witnessing more frequent heatwaves and a declining northeast monsoon reliability, crucial for agricultural productivity. Erratic rainfall, characterized by fewer but more intense rainfall events, exacerbates both flooding in coastal areas and water scarcity in interior regions. Agriculture, predominantly rice cultivation in the Cauvery Delta and other regions, faces critical impacts. Water-intensive crops like rice and sugarcane are under stress due to inconsistent water availability. Frequent drought conditions, particularly acute during failed monsoon years, have adversely affected yields and overall agricultural productivity. Coastal agriculture is further vulnerable to rising sea levels and salinity intrusion, adversely affecting soil fertility and crop productivity. Adaptation in Tamil Nadu involves adopting drought-resilient crop varieties, water conservation methods, and precision agriculture technologies. The state government has been actively promoting System of Rice Intensification (SRI), micro-irrigation techniques, and alternate cropping patterns involving less water-intensive crops such as pulses, millets, and oilseeds. Enhanced water resource management through watershed programs and rainwater harvesting structures remains critical. Integrated coastal zone management practices, including salinity-tolerant crop cultivation and mangrove restoration, also form essential adaptation measures. Strengthening farmer support mechanisms, expanding agricultural insurance, and promoting community-based climate awareness are vital strategies to sustain Tamil Nadu's agricultural productivity amid climate variability.

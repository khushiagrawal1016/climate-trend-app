Hotspot 1: Maharashtra

Detailed Climate Analysis and Impact on Agriculture

Regional Climate Analysis

Maharashtra has been experiencing notable climate variability characterized by increased temperatures, unpredictable rainfall, and frequent droughts. Temperature records indicate a rising trend, with heatwaves becoming more intense and frequent, especially in regions such as Vidarbha and Marathwada. Rainfall patterns have significantly altered, marked by delayed monsoons and concentrated precipitation events leading to floods in western Maharashtra, contrasted by prolonged dry spells in the eastern districts. The agricultural sector, which significantly contributes to the state’s economy, is severely impacted by these climatic shifts. Predominantly rainfed agriculture faces reduced productivity and frequent crop failures, especially for staple crops such as cotton, sugarcane, pulses, and millets. Extended drought conditions in Marathwada and Vidarbha have escalated agrarian distress, causing socio-economic hardships and farmer suicides due to mounting debts and crop losses. Adaptation strategies for Maharashtra primarily focus on enhancing water resource management and promoting sustainable agricultural practices. Initiatives such as watershed management, farm ponds, and micro-irrigation (drip and sprinkler systems) have been encouraged. Diversification towards drought-tolerant crops, agroforestry, and integrated farming systems can mitigate vulnerability. Government interventions through crop insurance schemes, climate-resilient seed distribution, and capacity-building programs for farmers to adopt climate-smart agriculture practices are crucial. Strengthening agricultural extension services, weather forecasting accuracy, and developing robust market linkages can further bolster the resilience of Maharashtra’s agriculture sector against climate-induced risks.
